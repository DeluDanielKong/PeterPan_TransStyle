6. KEY FINDINGS & CONCLUSION

   (a) The Chi2 score distribution exhibits a strong long-tail
       pattern: the top 10% (34 features) account for
       52.5% of total Chi2 discriminative power.

   (b) Sensitivity analysis demonstrates that classification
       performance (accuracy, F1) is highly stable across the
       5-25% range. Reducing to 10% preserves near-peak
       performance while dramatically reducing dimensionality
       (from 335 to 34 features).

   (c) Naive Bayes - the classifier most sensitive to irrelevant
       features - reaches its peak accuracy at the 10% threshold
       and degrades *significantly* (paired t-test p < .01)
       when more features are added (>= 30%).

   (d) SVM and Logistic Regression show no statistically
       significant difference in per-fold accuracy between
       10% and any other tested threshold (all p > .05),
       confirming that the reduced feature set retains full
       discriminative information.

   (e) The 10% threshold therefore represents a principled
       trade-off: it lies at the boundary where feature
       importance transitions from highly discriminative to
       marginal (long-tail break), achieves peak performance
       on the most feature-sensitive classifier (NB), and
       is robust across all classifiers and metrics tested.
